The problem
You're not afraid of English. You're afraid of saying something wrong in front of people.
You say "um" and switch to something simpler. And then think of the right answer two minutes later.
You keep staying quiet — even when you know it. That feeling has a name – and it's not a language problem.
It's the fear of being judged for trying. And your English stays exactly where it is.
How it works

You don't study English. You use it — from the first minute. Every session is live, small-group conversation.

Inside each session — 2 hours
Live speaking. Every minute.
  • A short original text as a starting point
  • 10–12 phrases pulled from it — not to memorise, but to use in conversation
  • Three conversation activities: guided, freer, then fully yours
  • Every session builds on the last — new words and phrases stay with you
Between sessions
4 short tasks per week
  • Record yourself reading the text aloud
  • Answer questions about it in writing
  • Use this week's phrases in new sentences
  • One 60-second spoken reflection — recorded on your phone
Your progress
Week by week, it builds
  • Words from Week 1 come back in Week 2 — in a new conversation
  • By Week 4 you have 40+ phrases you actually use — not just know
  • Not studied. Used. That's the difference.
✦
Erik listens to every recording and leaves personal feedback for each participant — every week.
